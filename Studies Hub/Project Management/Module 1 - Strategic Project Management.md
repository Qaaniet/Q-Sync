### 1. Introduction

An important aspect that differentiates high-performing organizations from low-performing organizations is the ability to strategically align project management to the overarching goals of the organization. Effective alignment results in project maturity and higher levels of both project and organizational success. Over time, the role of projects within organizations has changed dramatically, shifting from command and control structures to more projectized organizations. This shift has created a need for standardized structures for managing these projects to be developed, and while the newer structures are not without fault, there is no doubt that they are an improvement on previous structures. These changes also reflect the major change in management thinking at a broader level, mainly based on the acknowledgement that for projects to be successful in the long term, they have to align with company goals and strategies.

### 2. Definitions

Before continuing, it is worth recapping some important definitions and distinctions that will come up in this module, as well as throughout the rest of the course:

- ***Project:** “A temporary endeavour undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result” (_PMBOK® Guide_, 2013, 3).

- ***Programme:** “A group of related projects, subsidiary programmes, and programme activities managed in a coordinated way to obtain benefits and control that are not available from managing them individually,” (_PMBOK® Guide_, 2013, 9). Programmes often include elements of ongoing work. 

- **Portfolio:** A group of “projects, programmes, subsidiary portfolios, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives,” (_PMBOK® Guide_, 2017, 27). 

- **Organisational Project Management:** “A framework in which portfolio, programme, and project management are integrated with organisational enablers in order to achieve strategic objectives” (_PMBOK® Guide_, 2017, 28).

  

Not all projects are part of a programme. Programmes are more inclusive than projects in that they are closely linked to ongoing operations. This also implies greater influence on the general management of the organisation. Programmes are endeavours developed in accordance with the organisation’s goal, vision, and strategic objectives. The portfolio encompasses all organisational projects and programmes. The number of portfolios an organisation has often depends on its size.
